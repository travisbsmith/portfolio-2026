A founder I work with sells nasal strips — fun designs, health-conscious market, solid SEO. During one of our sessions, she pulled up her Shopify Marketing → Attribution view and pointed to a line item she didn't expect: referral traffic from ChatGPT.

Not a lot of it. But real, buying-intent traffic from someone who asked an AI assistant about nasal strips and got directed to her store.

That was early 2026. AI search referrals are only going to grow. And right now, most Shopify stores aren't set up to capture any of them.

## How AI search actually works

When someone asks ChatGPT "what are the best nasal strips for sleeping," the model doesn't search the web in real time the way Google does. It pulls from its training data, plus live web results via Bing (for models that support web search), plus any pages that Perplexity or other AI engines have indexed and can retrieve.

The brands that get cited share common traits: they have clear, factual content that directly answers questions, their pages are structured in a way AI can parse, and they're mentioned in authoritative sources across the web.

This is different from traditional SEO in emphasis but similar in foundation. Fast-loading, well-structured, factually rich pages win on Google *and* in AI results.

## Five things to do in the next 30 days

**1. Add an llms.txt file**

Think of this as an AI-native sitemap. It tells AI crawlers what your store sells, which pages matter, and which to ignore (cart, checkout, account pages).

There are Shopify apps that handle this automatically: Arc (llms.txt Generator), LLM Rank, and autoLLMs all generate and maintain the file for you. Install one, configure your brand description and product summary, and you're done. This takes 15 minutes and the upside is real.

**2. Check your robots.txt**

Go to `your-store.myshopify.com/robots.txt`. Verify that none of the major AI crawlers are blocked:
- `GPTBot` (OpenAI / ChatGPT)
- `ClaudeBot` (Anthropic)
- `PerplexityBot`
- `Bingbot` (ChatGPT uses Bing for live web results)

Shopify's default allows all of these, but third-party apps sometimes modify robots.txt. Worth a 5-minute check.

**3. Rewrite your product descriptions in answer-first format**

AI models pull from the first 40-60 words of a product description. Most Shopify product copy is written in marketing voice — evocative, brand-forward, light on facts. That's fine for a human skimming a product page. It's useless to an AI trying to cite you as a source.

Rewrite your hero product description to lead with direct, factual statements: what it is, who it's for, what it does, the key specs. You can layer brand voice in after the facts. But the first sentence should answer the question, not tease it.

**4. Build a dedicated FAQ page**

This is one of the highest-leverage moves for AI search. AI assistants are question-answering machines. A FAQ page that directly answers common questions about your product category gives AI something to cite.

Target the questions people actually ask AI about your category:
- "Do [product type] actually work?"
- "What's the difference between [your product] and [competitor]?"
- "Is [product] safe for [use case]?"
- "What should I look for when buying [product type]?"

Write 2-3 sentence direct answers to each. Add FAQPage schema markup if you can (Shopify's theme editor supports this, or you can use an SEO app). The combination of clear answers + structured markup is a strong AI citation signal.

**5. Set a baseline for your AI traffic**

Go to Shopify Marketing → Attribution now and note your current sessions from `openai.com`, `perplexity.ai`, `claude.ai`, and `bing.com`. Screenshot it or note the numbers in a spreadsheet.

Check it in 30 days after implementing the above. The delta is your signal that it's working.

## Why this matters now, not later

The brands who own a topic in AI search today will be very hard to displace later — for the same reason that SEO rankings from 2010 were very hard to displace in 2015. AI models have inertia. They learn associations and reinforce them.

Every month you wait, competitors build content, get mentioned in sources AI indexes, and establish presence in model training data. This isn't a 2027 problem. It's happening right now in every product category with any search volume.

For a niche product brand, being one of the two or three brands that AI reliably cites is a durable competitive advantage. The window to earn that position inexpensively is open right now.
